THE PROBLEM
Creators didn’t get into this to manage files. 
They got into it to tell stories.
The average creator’s week: record, export, upload to a transcription tool, copy the transcript into a doc, edit video in one app, cut clips in another, write show notes manually, format a newsletter by hand, upload to YouTube, upload to Spotify, schedule social posts in a third tool, and hope nothing breaks between steps.
47 tools touched before a single piece of content publishes. 23 hours per week lost to workflow management instead of storytelling. WHAT THE BROADCAST LEADERS GOT RIGHT
Before building, study what already works.
Saga (SagaNews) treats the story as a planning unit first. Journalists pitch stories into a shared workspace. Everyone works on the same record in real time. The story holds everything, research, scripts, video, assets, social versions. One source. No confusion.

Wolftech (acquired by Avid, November 2024) adds operational control. Every story has a cost attached. Resource booking, equipment scheduling, and production planning live inside the story record. You know what each piece of content costs to produce before it publishes.

Cuez (NAB 2026) introduced the most creator-relevant innovation: modular content blocks. Every story breaks into reusable blocks, text, graphics, titles, media. Those blocks adapt to TV, web, and social without copy-paste between tools. AI handles headline generation and platform formatting.

The gap: all three are built for broadcasters with broadcaster budgets. This platform takes the same architecture, strips the enterprise scaffolding, adds creator monetization, and prices it at the SaaS tier where creators actually live.

Backblaze B2: 11 nines of data durability. Free egress to Cloudflare. Object Lock prevents modification or deletion. Fraction of AWS S3 cost.

LucidLink: mounts the archive as a virtual drive. Editors work directly in Premiere or DaVinci Resolve without downloading files. First frame under one second on a 4K timeline.

Serverless FFmpeg: FFmpeg is free. AWS Lambda charges milliseconds of compute. A 1-hour 4K transcode costs under $0.50. Zero idle server cost. Loudness normalization to EBU R128 and ATSC A/85 standards runs automatically as a mandatory QC gate on every file before delivery.

n8n: open-source workflow automation. 400+ pre-built connectors. Self-hostable. Priced per workflow execution, not per step. Handles the full orchestration layer for complex iterative content pipelines.
PostgreSQL append-only rights ledger: application-level enforcement preventing UPDATE or DELETE on committed records. UUID primary keys. SHA-256 hashing where each record’s hash is stored in the next, tamper-evident chain without blockchain infrastructure overhead.

Lago: MIT-licensed open-source engine. Metered billing, subscription management, invoice generation. Connected to Stripe for payment processing. Self-hosted at near-zero cost.

Cloudflare Stream plus R2: enterprise CDN coverage across 300+ cities at commodity pricing. R2 storage has zero egress fees. Multi-CDN failover scoring runs every 30 seconds, sub-500ms reroute when a provider degrades.

OpenTelemetry plus Grafana: full observability stack at near-zero cost. Glass-to-glass latency measurement from camera capture to viewer screen. Deploys at Phase 1, not Phase 5. Without it there is no performance baseline, no billing metering data, and no historical record to optimize against.

QUALITY CONTROL: AUTOMATED
Every output scanned automatically before delivery: black frames and frozen frames, lip-sync drift detectable to 15ms, visual artifacts from AI processing tools, loudness violations against ATSC A/85 and EBU R128 standards.

Detection logic: black frame detected above 99% confidence, auto-correct and trigger encoder restart. Lip-sync drift detected above 90% confidence, auto-adjust audio delay. Visual blockiness detected above 95% confidence, switch to backup. Ambiguous result below 85% confidence, route to human review with specific flag.

Every AI-generated output requires one human approval tap before it publishes. The creator stays in control. The platform handles execution.

VERSIONING AND ROLLBACK
When the pipeline generates nine platform variants from one story, each variant is a separate asset. If the AI produces a corrupt version, the question is: how fast can the last approved state be restored? Without versioning, the answer is re-run the entire pipeline, 45–90 minutes for long-form video. That failure mode destroys creator trust on day 30 of a production deployment.
Three rollback operations are available. Single-variant rollback: revert one platform output to the previous version without affecting the other eight, execution time milliseconds. Full story rollback: revert all nine variants to their last approved state simultaneously, execution time milliseconds. Pipeline rollback: if a model update produces systematically bad output across multiple stories, identify all affected variants by model version and trigger selective regeneration using previously validated parameters.

When QC confidence drops below 85%, the system flags the variant as pending review and keeps the prior approved version as the active delivery asset. The creator sees a review notification, not missing content.

RIGHTS AND MONETIZATION LEDGER
Every piece of content is intellectual property. Most creators have no systematic record of who licensed what, for how long, in which territory, and at what fee.
The rights ledger tracks every asset across four dimensions: territory, duration, media type, and compensation. Every sub-license grant, ownership transfer, and usage event is recorded as an immutable transaction. Nobody modifies a committed record.
Tables: rights_ledger tracks the master record of every asset’s rights status. transactions tracks every change in ownership or license. journal_entries tracks double-entry accounting where debits and credits sum to zero. licenses tracks territory, duration, format, and compensation per grant. usage_meter tracks API calls, compute usage, and delivery events.

FAST channel integration: the playout system schedules assets from the archive in a rolling 24/7 playlist, inserts ad break markers at defined intervals, and delivers a live stream to FAST distributors including Pluto TV, Tubi, Samsung TV Plus, and Roku Channel. The rights ledger verifies distribution clearance for each asset before it enters the schedule.

FIVE GAPS IN EVERY CUSTOM CREATOR STACK
These are the problems this platform solves that no existing tool addresses completely.
First: metadata contract violations. One system sends content type as one format, the next expects another. Assets stall silently. Fix: enforce a canonical metadata schema at ingest. Every component reads the same contract.
Second: loudness normalization treated as optional. It is not. Non-compliant audio is rejected by every professional ingest system. The platform runs it automatically on every asset before delivery.
Third: no versioning for AI-generated outputs. Without rollback, an AI pipeline is a demo, not a product.
Fourth: single CDN dependency. One provider goes down, everything goes down. Multi-CDN failover with automated scoring is baseline infrastructure, not a premium feature.
Fifth: observability deferred to later. Without OpenTelemetry deployed from day one, there is no baseline, no billing metering data, and no glass-to-glass measurement. It must deploy at Phase 1.
